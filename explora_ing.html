    Mexico ecosystems

    Tropical forest

    The rainforests grow in the tropics where it is hot all year. In some heavy rain and plants grow all the time, while others are dry seasons and plants lose their leaves.

    There are a variety of plants and small animals from ants and fungi to large predators like the jaguar and the harpy eagle.

    Cloud forest

    Cloud forest grows on the mountains above tropical rain forest and below temperate forests. As their name indicates, they are covered with clouds a good part of the year and it rains constantly.

    There is a great diversity of epiphytes, this is plants that grow on the trees without damaging them, such as orchids, bromeliads and woody vines. Because of the great humidity, this forest is home to many frogs, toads, salamanders and to a great variety of interesting species.

    Temperate forest

    The forests of pines, firs and oaks grow in the mountains where it is not too cold or hot and rains only part of the year.

    Plants and animals in these forests are much like the species of Canada and the United States, but there are also many species unique to Mexico.

    Rivers and lakes

    Up high in the mountains, small creeks are born, and little by little they become big rivers than end up in lakes, lagoons and in the sea.

    Many species of plants and animals, big and small, live in the water and in the shores. The large trees protect the river and maintain a stable temperature and many species enjoy the climate created by the water.

    Grassland

    In this ecosystem are abundant grasses and few shrubs and trees.

    As in the bushes during the day is hot and cold at night, so most animals are fed morning and evening.

    Scrubland

    In much of central and northern Mexico grow the Scrubland, sometimes called deserts. Here it is very hot during the day and cold at night and little rain.

    Plants and animals of the scrubland are adapted to these changes in temperature. Here live many cacti and plants with tiny leaves.

    Dry forest

    Tropical dry forests grow on hot places where the seasons are marked. During the rainy season, the forest is lush green but in the dry season, when there is no rain, plants lose their leaves and grow lots of flowers.

    The short trees have small leaves and because there is a lot of light coming in, the place is full of shrubs and small trees. It is difficult to walk through these forests. Many tree species are relatives of copal and legumes, and from their higher branches, the long nests of orioles hang down.

    Pacific Ocean

    The Pacific Ocean is cold and can be very deep and its coasts are drier than those of the Gulf and Caribbean.

    Here live a lot of marine plants and animals and many other islands and coasts.

    There are microscopic animals and as large as whales.

    Gulf and the Caribbean

    The Gulf and the Caribbean have warmer water and are shallower and it´s coasts are wetter than those of the Pacific Ocean.

    There are many mangroves and coral reefs, home to many plants and animals.

    Countryside

    All over the countryside there are areas where people produce vegetables, fruit, cattle, sheep, chickens and many other products that are transported to the city to feed its inhabitants.

    Lots of fruit and vegetables originated in Mexico, such as maize, pumpkin, chilli peppers, cocoa, and vanilla. Others, such as lettuce, cabbage, wheat, coffee, mango and apple, were brought from other continents. The turkey was domesticated in Mexico, but cows, sheep, pigs, goats and chickens come from Europe and Asia.

    City

    The cities are dominated by houses, buildings, streets and cars, but above all by one species: humans. There are few plants and animals adapted to these conditions and many of them come from other countries.

    Lots of trucks carry food from the countryside and others remove the waste that we produce. The water and energy we use as electricity also comes from afar.

    Jaguar

    Soy el mamífero depredador más grande de la selva. Mis presas no tienen chance. Las puedo atrapar en los árboles, en el suelo o en el agua, durante el día o la noche. Me alimento de mamíferos, aves y reptiles. Puedo capturar animales tan grandes como un cocodrilo y tan pequeños como un ratón.
