    What is a species?

    On our planet there are many kinds of plants, animals, fungi, bacteria and other organisms so small they are almost invisible. Some are very familiar and others are little known.

    Each one of the plants and animals that we can name, such as dog, cat, daisy, magnolia, corn, rose, pineapple, strawberry, green iguana, jaguar, wolf, swamp crocodile, Kemp’s Ridley turtle and drunken toad, are different species.

    Each species has different needs in order to live, feed, grow and reproduce. Some species (plants) are directly dependent on energy from the sun, while other species (animals) depend on plants or other animals for energy

    Equal, but different?

    Individuals of the same species of plant, animal, fungus or microorganism can be very different in size, form, color, function and behavior. It is the same with your companions, friends, parents, brothers and sisters; they can be tall, short, thin, fat, dark and fair.

    These differences transmitted from parents to children are easy to recognize among the great variety of species of domesticated plants and animals, such as maize, tomato, chili, marigold, poinsettia, cows, dogs and cats, and the differences exist in all of the species on the planet.

    Native and exotic

    We can distinguish two native and two exotic groups , according to their distribution.

    Natives

    Native species

    These species have their origin in Mexico, but can also live in other countries (e.g. golden eagle, jaguar, monarch butterfly, blue whale).


    Endemic species

    Some species only live in Mexico and are known as endemic (volcano rabbit, gulf porpoise). They can be restricted to one river, mountain or lake, or may be found all over a state.


    Exotics

    Exotic species

    Exotic species are those that do not originate in Mexico. Some were brought to Mexico to satisfy our needs for food (e.g. cows, horses, donkeys, hens, fishes, wheat, banana, coffee, bees), or for other reasons (e.g. eucalyptus, bougainvillea, jacaranda, African tulip, cats and dogs).


    Invasive species

    Some species are introduced from other countries, either on purpose or accidently, on boats, airplanes or cars and can become a real problem since they compete, predate, parasitize, transmit diseases or alter the habitat of the native species (e.g. rat, cockroach, pigeon, suckermouth catfish , lily).

    Latin names

    Each species has a scientific name composed of two parts. Scientific names are written in Latin so that all countries use the same name. The first part is the genus and tells us which group the species belongs to. The second part tells us which species a specimen belongs to. They are like your surname and forename.

    For example, the jaguar is called Panthera onca , the leopard is Panthera pardus, the African lion is Panthera leo and the tiger is Panthera tigris. The first name tells us that they are all closely related.

    On the other hand, the puma is Puma concolor and the cat is Felis silvestris catus. Even though we know that both belong to the cat family, the name tells us they are not as closely related as the jaguar, leopard and lion.
