    Exploradors

    In our exciting trip throughout Megadiverse Mexico, we discovered that our country is home to many species of animals and plants. We can find them in different ecosystem. Many species are similar to each other and it is very interesting to learn how to tell them apart.

    Observing and identifying plants and animals is a fun activity. We can do it in small groups. I will give you some recommendations to help you observe many species without disturbing their environment.

    1

    Before going out, get ready! Find your back pack with binoculars, notebook, pen or pencil and any guide to identify species.

    2

    Do not use perfumes or cosmetic products. Animals could easily smell and hide from you.

    3

    Walk lightly without making noise. Many species get scare with the sound of our voice and the noise of our footsteps.

    4

    Whisper to you companions or make hand signals if you want to alert them of something you have seen.

    5

    Take pictures, they are a record of what you have seen and will help you to identify the plants and animals.

    6

    Enjoy and share with your Friends what you have observed. You can do it in NaturaLista www.naturalista.mx

    Birds

    Many birds are active during the day and sing. This makes them easier to identify. You only need good sight or binoculars, good ear, a notebook to write your observations, and a bird field guide.

    There are many types of bird field guides. Most of them have illustrations or photographs, the place where they live and a brief description. You can start with Manual para principiantes en la observación de aves.

    What you need
    to observe to identify a bird:

    1

    Size. Birds can be large like a crane or small like a hummingbird.

    2

    Body shape. Look at the body shape, head size in relation to body, long or short legs, wing shape, and you will be able to identify the group where the bird belongs.

    3

    Beak shape. It will help you to identify the group and their food habits.

    4

    Tail shape. The tail is the rudder of flight. There are many types depending on flying habits.

    5

    Body colors. You can observe the colors in different parts of the body, head, throat, chest, wings, tail, eyes, and even in their feet.

    Butterflies

    Butterflies are a group of insects with amazing shapes and colors. To identify species you need to have your eyes wide open and a photographic memory, or a camera. A small line or dot in the wings can make the difference between the species.

    What you need
    to observe to identify butterflies.

    1

    Size. There are very small butterflies like the skippers and also big ones and colorful.

    2

    Wing shape. You need to find out if the wings are rounded, triangular, pointy, or if they have swallow trails in in the back wings.

    3

    Wing color. There are many colors, bands, spots, figures. Each characteristic is like the butterfly finger print and they are very important to differentiate them. Furthermore, the sides of the wings are completely different in some butterflies.

    4

    Flight behavior and landing position. Some butterflies move their wings rapidly while others glide. Some close their wings immediately as they land on a flower.

    Trees

    Trees produce oxygen, food and wood. There are many species of trees, some of them we have planted in cities, others are important for their fruits, we also obtain Wood from some and others are used as Christmas Trees. In the city, those neighborhoods with trees are cozier than the ones without them.

    What you need
    to observe to identify trees.

    1

    Height. Trees can grow up to 80 meters tall in the rain forest. This is the height of a 30 store building.

    2

    Leave type. There are many types of leaves: simple, they have only leave per stalk, or compound, with two or more leaflets borne on the same leafstalk. Their shape is also very diverse, they can be smooth and shiny or hairy and drab, with toothed or smooth margins, with complex or simple leaf nervation, thick or thin. They can have many shapes: oval, elongated, like star or feather.

    3

    Flowers. Each species of tree has a particular flower. There are many colors, sizes and shapes.

    4

    Fruits. Not all tree fruits are edible, but we can use the fruits to identify them. Fruits come rounded, flat, small, big and colorful. Look around the base of the tree to find fallen flowers, fruits or seeds.

    5

    Bark. Bark can be smooth or rough. They can also have different colors. Green, brown, red or white. Some trees have unique characteristics such as thorns or their bark peels off.

    Mammals

    Human beings belong to the group of mammals. Most mammals are difficult to observe in the wild since most of them are nocturnal, and secretive and slippery. Mammals are unique for feeding milk to their young and for having hair. There are minuscule mammals like shrews and huge ones like the blue whale.

    Even if we cannot see them because they are nocturnal, we can identify their presence by indirect observations such as tracks, or pellets left behind.

    What you need to observe
    to identify mammals by their tracks.

    1

    Footprint size and depth. These characteristics tell us about the size and weight of an animal.

    2

    Number and shape of fingers. Count the number of fingers and how they are arranged. Each mammal has a different footprint. Some mark 3, 4 or 5 fingers.

    3

    Hooves. These are modified fingers of some mammalian species and there are also different between species.

    4

    Presence of nails and size. There are long and thin nails that leave only a point as a mark on the soil or thick nails that can leave a stronger print.

    5

    Differences between hands and feet. Hand prints and footprints are different. Footprints are usually larger.

    Recording the track

    Explorers can recognize many animals by observing signals that we find on the pathways, for example, half eaten fruits, hair, tracks, pellets, and dens. If we find a track we need to record its size and shape in our field notebook.

    We will show you how to replicate animal tracks with plaster molds (cast). You can make a very interesting collection from the footprints of different animals.

    Materials: Plaster, water, plastic recipient, spatula y cardboard.

    1

    Carefully clean the area around
    the footprints from sticks and
    stones that may be on the way.

    2

    Surround the footprint with a long (45 cm)
    and thin (3 cm) piece of cardboard as
    if you were using a mold to cut cookies.

    3

    Mix two parts of plaster and one of water in a flexible plastic recipient. You can make a recipient cutting an old plastic soccer football in half.

    4

    Pour the mix continuously on the mold and let it dry for 15 or 20 minutes.

    5

    Release the dry plaster off the cardboard mold. We have now a cast with positive image of the track.

    6

    On the back of the cast write the date, place and name of the animal.

    With this cast you can begin a great collection!
