    Before we arrived in Mexico

    Before we arrived in Mexico, jungles, forests, shrubland and pasture covered the whole country.

    7000 A.C.

    7000 B.C.

    The beginning of agriculture. The first settlers domesticate maize, beans, squash and hunter-gatherers become farmers. This was the origin of the first scattered settlements and the origin of milpa.

    People

    Cities, agriculture and livestock production

    Ecosystems

    1,500 A.D.

    1,500 A.D.

    The Spanish arrive in Mexico and bring livestock with them. Cows, sheep, goats, horses, donkeys, pigs, and chickens are introduced into the country. Products, such as coffee, bananas and rice, begin to arrive from other countries. A large number of indigenous people are killed by war and new diseases. Of more than 20 million, only 2 million remain alive.

    2 million people

    1,900 A.D.

    1,900 A.D.

    Agriculture increases on large estates dedicated to the export of products such as livestock, cotton, henequen, gum, coffee, tobacco, banana, guayule, fine woods, sugar and rice. Some estates produce milk, wheat and maize for local consumption.

    14 million people

    Cities, agriculture and livestock production

    Ecosystems

    1,950 A.D.

    1,950 A.D.

    For over 500 years there were fewer than 20 million people in Mexico. In the past 50 years the population has doubled. Ejidos and indigenous communities produce maize, beans and other basic commodities. Small farms make use of new agricultural and irrigation technologies. New breeds of cattle are introduced to the tropics such as the zebu.

    26 million people

    2,010 A.D.

    A2,010 A.D.

    The population has increased almost fourfold over the past 60 years. Large areas of tropical forest in Veracruz and Tabasco have been destroyed to be replaced with pastureland for livestock. As well as there being more people, consumption and waste increase.

    103 million people

    2,050

    2,050

    People worked for a better future. They reduced their consumption and waste of meat, water, and fuel and consumed more vegetables and grains in a sustainable way. People restored forests, shrublands, grasslands which are now recovering.
