    What damages ecosystems?

    Direct actions that damage and threaten species and their ecosystems are: Conversion of ecosystems into agricultural zones, pasture for livestock, dams, urban areas and roads. Excessive exploitation of species, including illegal trafficking. Introduction of species from other countries that become invaders. Pollution of soil, water and air.

    Tropical forest

    The main direct impacts are the transformation of the ecosystem for the purposes of agriculture and livestock production. Climate change caused by greenhouse gases produces warmer and drier conditions that affect humid ecosystems.

    Livestock production

    Pastures for raising cattle and other animals for food have replaced the tropical forests in many places.

    Roads

    Roads fragment the tropical forests and certain species, such as the spider and howler monkeys, are left isolated.

    Forest

    The main threats to the forest are destruction and deterioration as a result of illegal logging, fires, clearing for agriculture, livestock production, urban development and roads. The slow recovery, reduced distribution and constant disturbance of the forest have caused them to be considered fragile, and destined for disappearance. For this reason, they are a priority for conservation.

    Climate change

    Glaciers on the volcanoes have melted due to global warming. We are losing the sources of water that refill our aquifers and springs and supply us with the water we use every day .

    Fires

    Most fires are provoked by us, either accidentally or intentionally. When we burn grasslands to promote the growth of grasses, fires escape and affect other ecosystems.

    Grassland

    The pastureland is one of the most threatened environments in North America. It is being replaced by agricultural and livestock production zones. Overgrazing by cattle, sheep and goats causes the loss of plant and animal species, as well as the soil.

    Urbanization

    Unplanned growth of cities extends first, through flat areas where grasslands are located, erasing them completely.

    Agriculture

    The first ecosystems to be replaced by agriculture are the grasslands located in flat areas. Sometimes exotic grasses are introduced to feed the cattle.

    Scrubland

    Overgrazing by cattle, sheep and goats has caused large changes in the composition of the scrublands. The plant species preferred by the herbivores disappear while populations of indigestible species increase.

    Overexploitation

    The illegal extraction of cactus species in Mexico has placed various species in danger of extinction.

    Pollution

    Large amounts of garbage are thrown in shrublands, producing different types of pollution.

    Pacific Ocean

    The lack of care and panoramic vision has been one of the main causes of coastal degradation in recent decades. Hotels have been eliminating important coastal zones and affecting mangroves, corals and marine grasses.

    Refusal

    Large amounts of solid and liquid waste is discarded into the sea by coastal industries, touristic zones, cities and towns.

    Gulf and the Caribbean

    The main threats are hotel development as well as transformation and pollution by agriculture, livestock production, domestic waste and oil spillage.

    Coral bleaching

    Increased seawater temperatures caused by climate change kills the algae and coral polyps, causing the visible bleaching of the coral.

    Invasive species

    Pets released in places where they do not belong compete with, predate upon and transform the environment of the native species, as well as transmitting diseases.
