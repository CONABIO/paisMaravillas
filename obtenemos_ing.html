    What do we obtain from ecosystems?

    For a long time we have benefited from air, water, soil and natural remedies without paying for their cost. When we transform ecosystems, their environmental systems become scarce and we have to pay for them. All the food, materials and objects that we use every day, come from ecosystems.

    Tropical forest

    This ecosystem contains a great variety of species of plants and animals. It captures the water and also the carbon dioxide that helps to combat climate change and contributes to the formation and conservation of the soil.

    Melipona bees

    These bees are native to Mexico, have no sting and their honey has medicinal properties.

    Cocoa

    A Mexican plant that produces the seeds from which chocolate is obtained.

    Chicle

    This is used as a chewing gum and is obtained from the tree called Chicozapote.

    Maguey

    Fibers from the maguey are used to decorate leather goods such as belts.

    Palm roof

    The leaves of the guano palm are used as a house roofing material.

    Forest

    From the forests, we get a large part of our timber and fibers for making paper. The forest absorbs water like a sponge and releases it little by little. Forests provide oxygen, fertile soils and help prevent erosion. They also reduce pollution and the greenhouse effect. Pine nuts: These are the seeds of pine trees and they contain a lot of energy and nutrients. They can be eaten toasted or raw.

    Runoff

    Movement of water through the landscape provides many benefits, such as a clean supply of water, soil formation, ecosystem growth and public health.

    Montezuma pine

    The wood of this tree is used in construction and also as a fuel. Its leaves, known as ocochal, are used to make handcrafts.

    Pine nuts

    These are the seeds of pine trees and they contain a lot of energy and nutrients. They can be eaten toasted or raw.

    Resin

    Pine resin is used in the production of paper, adhesives, paints, varnishes, enamels, fishing nets and cleaning products.

    Blackberries

    The leaves of the blackberry plant are used to cure sores and skin injuries. Its fruits are particularly rich in vitamins A and C.

    Grassland

    Most of the natural pasturelands have already been transformed by agriculture and livestock production.

    Candelilla

    The candelilla plant has medicinal properties. Its juice is used as a remedy for different ailments.

    Candelilla wax

    This wax is used in the fabrication of cosmetics, crayons, paints, candles, coverings for fruit and matches.

    Mesquite

    The leaves of the mesquite plant are used medicinally, the seeds are milled to make flour, jam or wine and its dry branches are used as a fuel in ovens.

    Sotol

    An alcoholic drink similar to tequila is obtained from the sotol plant.

    Scrubland

    The nopals and magueys are scrubland plants from which we obtain a large quantity of products.

    Maguey worm

    These butterfly larvae grow in the roots of the maguey plant and are used in Mexican cuisine.

    Istle

    A textile fiber obtained from the maguey and used to make rope, bags and shoes.

    Medicines

    In the scrublands, there are medicinal plants such as the creosote bush that is used as a remedy for kidney problems.

    Pulque

    A prehispanic alcoholic drink made from fermented maguey juice.

    Pacific Ocean

    The oceans are the great producers of the planet. Their microscopic flora (phytoplankton) provides 90% of the oxygen in the atmosphere. The terrestrial flora produces the other 10%.

    Oxygen

    The microscopic algae that float adrift on the sea are responsible for large quantities of the oxygen on Earth.

    Fish

    The Pacific Ocean is the main producer of fish for human consumption.

    Salts

    Sea salt, is very appreciated in food and gastronomy. It comes from the evaporation of sea water.

    Tourism

    The grey whales that spend the winter in Mexican waters are a great tourist attraction.

    Gulf and the Caribbean

    The mangroves are nurseries for many of the species of fish that we eat.

    Coral reef

    A wide diversity of mollusks, worms , crustaceans and fish live in the reef.

    Petroleum

    Petroleum is the result of communities of organisms that existed millions of years ago.

    Countryside

    Agricultural and livestock fields produce vegetables, grains, cereals , fruit, milk, meat, wool, eggs and many other products that are transported to the city to feed the inhabitants.

    Amaranth

    Amaranth was a food of great importance to the prehispanic inhabitants of Mexico. It is highly nutritious.

    Chili

    The chili is also originally from Mexico. It has been one of the main ingredients of Mexican cuisine since ancient times.

    Maize

    Mexico is the center of origin and domestication of maize. There are currently around 60 different varieties cultivated in the country.

    Cheese

    Milk, cheese, cream and other derived products were added to the Mexican diet with the arrival of the Spaniards.

    City

    Human beings, buildings, streets and factories predominate in the cities. Cities consume many products that come from afar and produce wastes and garbage that also travel a long distance.

    Waste

    Cities produce a large amount of waste that has to be transported long distances for disposal.

    Knowledge

    Cities also concentrate and distribute knowledge.

    Culture

    Cities concentrate a large quantity of cultural activities of many different origins.

    Housing

    The dwellings of human beings are of many different shapes and colors.
