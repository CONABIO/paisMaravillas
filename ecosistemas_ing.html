What is an ecosystem?

An ecosystem is a place where plants and animals live together with their environment. We are always in an ecosystem and are always part of it.




At breakfast we use plants (wheat, maize, cocoa) and animals (eggs, milk, meat) and to wash we use water from rivers and lakes. In the city ecosystem many products come from far away.



Do you know where the bananas that you ate for breakfast come from?

Variety of ecosystems

In Mexico there are a variety of ecosystems such as marine, coastal, forest, jungle, thicket, grassland, lakes, rivers and streams.











In each you will find different plants and animals,
but similar relationships exist in all of them.

Producers-Consumers-Decomposers

Some species trap the energy from the sun while others get the energy from those species.

Consumers

Animals are known as "consumers" because they do not produce their own energy, but instead take it from plants or other animals.

Producers

Plants are known as "producers" because they can capture energy from the sun and transform it into chemical energy.

Decomposers

Microorganisms take back materials to the soil and are called decomposers.
